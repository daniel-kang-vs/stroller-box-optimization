BabyShip
Packaging Optimization

BabyShip ships 17 top-selling strollers nationwide. Carriers charge on dimensional weight (L×W×H ÷ 250), so box choice directly drives shipping cost — but warehouses can’t stock a unique box for every product.

The Problem

Bulky gear + DIM pricing + limited box catalog

Strollers vary widely in size, but carriers bill on whichever is larger: actual weight or dimensional weight. The optimization finds the best 7 boxes (from 43 candidates) that cover all 17 products while minimizing total billable weight.

Height drives DIM weight for most SKUs — two outliers (Vista V2, Fox 5) force oversized boxes.

Optimization Model

A box-assignment integer program

The model chooses which box types to keep in the warehouse and which box each stroller uses. It minimizes total billable shipping weight while enforcing fit rules and limiting the active box catalog to 7 box types.

01 · Sets

Products and Candidate Boxes

The model evaluates every product-box pair, then chooses a small box catalog that can cover all products.

\[ \begin{aligned} I &= \{\text{candidate boxes}\} \\ J &= \{\text{stroller products}\} \end{aligned} \]
02 · Decision Variables

Assignment + Box Selection

\(x_{ij}\) assigns product \(j\) to box \(i\). \(y_i\) turns a box type on or off in the warehouse catalog.

\[ \begin{aligned} x_{ij} &\in \{0,1\} \quad \text{product } j \text{ uses box } i \\ y_i &\in \{0,1\} \quad \text{box } i \text{ is stocked} \end{aligned} \]
03 · Objective Function

Minimize Total Billable Weight

The assignment cost is the carrier billable weight: the larger of actual product weight and box dimensional weight.

\[ \begin{aligned} \min_{x,y} \quad & \sum_{i \in I}\sum_{j \in J} c_{ij}x_{ij} \\ c_{ij} = & \max\left(w_j,\frac{L_i W_i H_i}{250}\right) \end{aligned} \]
04 · Constraints

One Assignment, Seven Boxes, Feasible Fit

Each product gets one box, products can only use active box types, and the active catalog is capped at seven boxes.

\[ \begin{aligned} \sum_{i \in I} x_{ij} &= 1 && \forall j \in J \\ x_{ij} &\le y_i && \forall i \in I,\; j \in J \\ \sum_{i \in I} y_i &\le 7 \\ x_{ij} &= 0 && \text{if product } j \text{ does not fit in box } i \end{aligned} \]

Optimal Solution

Integer program solved with Gurobi in 0.02s

Each product is assigned exactly one box. At most 7 box types can be active. The solver picks assignments that minimize total billable weight subject to fit rules (product + 0.5″ padding must fit inside the box).

Products per Box Type

S-16765 (36×24×18) is the workhorse — covers 5 of 17 products. Box 8 (40×40×40) handles the two heaviest outliers.

Impact

What standardization costs

We compared the 7-box system against a “perfect fit” baseline where every product gets its own ideal box. The penalty is only 6.5% extra billable weight — a small trade for cutting packaging SKUs by more than half.
