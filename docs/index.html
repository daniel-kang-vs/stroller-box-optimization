BabyShip
Packaging Optimization

BabyShip ships 17 top-selling strollers nationwide. Carriers charge on dimensional weight (L×W×H ÷ 250), so box choice directly drives shipping cost — but warehouses can’t stock a unique box for every product.

The Problem

Bulky gear + DIM pricing + limited box catalog

Strollers vary widely in size, but carriers bill on whichever is larger: actual weight or dimensional weight. The optimization finds the best 7 boxes (from 43 candidates) that cover all 17 products while minimizing total billable weight.

Product Dimensions (inches)

Height drives DIM weight for most SKUs — two outliers (Vista V2, Fox 5) force oversized boxes.

Billable Weight: DIM vs. Actual

Blue = what the carrier charges. Gray = product weight. When blue is taller, you pay for empty box space.

17 Unique Boxes vs. 7 Shared

One box per product is cheapest to ship but hard to operate. Seven shared types cut SKUs by 59%.

Optimal Solution

Integer program solved with Gurobi in 0.02s

Each product is assigned exactly one box. At most 7 box types can be active. The solver picks assignments that minimize total billable weight subject to fit rules (product + 0.5″ padding must fit inside the box).

Products per Box Type

S-16765 (36×24×18) is the workhorse — covers 5 of 17 products. Box 8 (40×40×40) handles the two heaviest outliers.

Impact

What standardization costs

We compared the 7-box system against a “perfect fit” baseline where every product gets its own ideal box. The penalty is only 6.5% extra billable weight — a small trade for cutting packaging SKUs by more than half.
